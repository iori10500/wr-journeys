Zhangjiajie National Forest Park · Golden Whip Stream & Yellow Stone Village
Full day in the Zhangjiajie National Forest Park (UNESCO World Heritage core zone) with a private English-speaking guide. Morning: hike the Golden Whip Stream — a 7.5 km flat trail winding along a crystalline creek through towering sandstone pillars, with wild macaques likely spotted along the way. The day Zhangjiajie was made for. D5
Day 5
Tianmen Mountain · Glass Skywalk · Heaven's Gate
Drive ~40 minutes to Zhangjiajie city. Board the world's longest cable car (7,455m, ~30 minutes) rising through cloud layers to the summit of Tianmen Mountain — the ride itself is an attraction. Walk the Glass Skywalk, a transparent walkway cantilevered from the cliff face at 1,400m altitude (bring your nerve). Visit Tianmen Cave (Heaven's Gate) — a 130m-high natural arch in the mountain; climb the 999 steps or take the escalator to stand beneath the arch and peer through at open sky. Return to Wulingyuan; farewell Tujia ethnic dinner before tomorrow's departure.

Day 6
Zhangjiajie → Fenghuang Ancient Town · First Evening
Optional morning stop at a Tujia minority cultural village en route. Private car ~2.5 hours through the Xiangxi highlands to Fenghuang Ancient Town — a 1,300-year-old riverside settlement of stilted wooden houses, stone bridges, and lantern-lit alleyways, widely considered one of China's most photogenic small towns. Check in to a boutique riverside inn with direct Tuojiang River views. Evening walking tour: cross the stepping-stone bridges, follow the old city walls, and watch the town transform after dark as thousands of red lanterns ignite over the water. Dinner at a riverside restaurant.

Day 7
Fenghuang In Depth · Miao Silverwork · Evening Punt
Early morning in the old town before 8am — empty streets, golden light, the town as it was. Visit the former residence of Shen Congwen, China's most beloved country-life novelist, born here; his novella Border Town made Fenghuang famous throughout the Chinese-speaking world. Explore the East Gate Tower and old city walls. Afternoon: step into a Miao silversmith workshop for a custom silver piece made while you watch. As evening falls, board a traditional wooden sampan for a night punt along the Tuojiang — drifting under ancient bridges while the lanterns multiply on the water above and below. The evening that stays with you longest.

Natural Wonders
Hunan's Hidden Landscapes
Four places · Four kinds of extraordinary
Wulingyuan Sandstone Pillars
Over 3,000 quartz-sandstone columns, some exceeding 1,000 metres, perpetually wreathed in cloud and mist — the real-world landscape that inspired Avatar's Hallelujah Mountains. UNESCO World Heritage Site.
Tianmen Mountain Glass Skywalk
A transparent walkway cantilevered from the cliff at 1,400m altitude with nothing but air beneath your feet. Reached by the world's longest cable car (7.5 km) — the approach is as dramatic as the destination.
Fenghuang Ancient Town
China's finest preserved Miao-minority riverside town, 1,300 years old. Stilted wooden houses line both banks of the Tuojiang River — immortalised by novelist Shen Congwen in Border Town.
The Tuojiang River at Night
As darkness falls, thousands of red lanterns illuminate the stilt houses and their mirror image in the water below. Best experienced from a slow wooden punt drifting under ancient stone bridges.
Travel Notes
Practical Information
What to know before you go
✈ Flights
Singapore (SIN) → Changsha Huanghua (CSX). Direct ~5 hrs via Scoot or China Southern. Multiple daily departures. Book 2–3 months ahead for October — prices rise sharply closer to the date.
📅 Best Travel Window
October 9–26 (post-Golden Week). Temperatures 18–25°C, low humidity, clear skies with morning mist (which enhances Zhangjiajie dramatically). Crowds drop sharply after Oct 7. Avoid Oct 1–7 entirely.
📋 Visa
Most Western passport holders require a Chinese tourist visa (L visa). Singapore residents apply through the Chinese Visa Application Service Centre — typically 4–5 business days. Several EU nationalities now enjoy 15-day visa-free entry; verify current policy for your specific passport.
📱 Connectivity
Purchase a China mainland SIM card on arrival. WhatsApp, Google, and Instagram require a VPN — configure yours before departure, as apps cannot be downloaded inside China. Alipay / WeChat Pay are ubiquitous; carry some cash RMB for small vendors in Fenghuang.
🚗 Getting Around
All inter-city transfers by high-speed rail or private car. Within each destination, your private driver is on call. All cable cars, elevators, and park transport included. No shared coaches or group buses on this itinerary.
👗 What to Wear
October mountain temperatures range 18–25°C by day, dropping to 10–12°C at night — a light down or fleece layer is essential. Bring proper walking shoes for the park trails. The Tianmen Glass Skywalk may close in strong wind or dense fog; we build in flexibility for this.
